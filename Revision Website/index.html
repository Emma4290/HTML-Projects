Particle Theory

Density

Density is a measure of how much matter is in an object. It is measured in Kg/m³ or g/cm³.

Density is calculated using the equation:

Density = mass / volume

  • Density is measured in Kg/m³ or g/cm³.
  • Mass is measured in Kg or g.
  • Volume is measured in m³ cm³.

The Particle Model

There are 3 types of matter:

  1. Solid
  2. Liquid
  3. Gas

Solids are arranged closely packed together in orderly lines and can only vibrate about a fixed position.

Liquid particles still touch but are able to slide and flow past eachother.

Gas particles are able to move freely. They will fill the available space.

Energy

Energy comes in several different forms:

  1. Magnetic
  2. Kinetic
  3. Heat
  4. Light
  5. Gravitational Potential
  6. Chemical
  7. Sound
  8. Elastic
  9. Electrical
  10. Nuclear

They can be remember using the mnemomic Most Kids Hate Learning GCSE Energy Names.

The principle of Conservation of Energy states that cannot be created or destroyed, only changed from one form to another.

Two important equations are below:

Kinetic Energy

Kinetic Energy relates to motion of an object.

Kinetic Energy = ½ x mass x velocity²

  • KE is measured in Joules, J.
  • Mass is measured in Kg or g.
  • Velocity is measured in metres per second, m/s.
Gravitational Potential Energy

Gravitational Potential Energy relates to the position of an object above the Earth's surface.

Gravitational Potential Energy = mass x gravity x height

  • GPE is measured in Joules, J.
  • Mass is measured in Kg or g.
  • Gravity is measured in Newtons per kilogram, N/Kg. For GCSE, gravity on Earth can be assumed to be 10N/Kg.
  • Height is measured in metres, m.

Radioactivity

There are 3 types of radiation:

  1. Alpha
  2. Beta
  3. Gamma

They are made up as follows:

  • Alpha particles are the same as a Helium nucleus and are made up of two protons and two neutrons. they have a mass of 4 and a positive charge of 2+.
  • Beta particles are small, negatively charged, fast-moving electrons.
  • Gamma radiation is a high energy wave.

Forces

Newton's First Law of motion states that every object will remain at rest or in uniform motion in a straight line unless compelled to change its state by the action of an external force.

Newton's Second Law is an equation:

Force = mass x acceleration

  • Force is measured in Newtons, N.
  • Mass is measured in Kg or g.
  • Acceleration is measured in m/s²

Newton's Third Law of Motion states that every action will have an equal and opposite reaction.
